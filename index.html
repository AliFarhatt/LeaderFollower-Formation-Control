Project Meeting Summary

Leader–Follower Formation Control
Visual LOS Without External Localization

Project Overview

Main project focus Visual line-of-sight leader–follower formation without external localization
Most critical technical gap Reliable relative pose estimation under intermittent visual measurements
Key practical decision Marker-based setup vs. markerless model-based pose estimation

1 Prior Work by Ali Shokor

FYP: Single-drone visual tracking

Ali's FYP focused on a drone that detects an object with an onboard camera and follows it. The target was a pig, and the work was fully simulated in SITL . SITL is manageable when only one drone is involved.

  • Target is visually detected and kept in frame
  • Drone follows the detected target using camera feedback
  • Setup is closer to object tracking than to formation control

Thesis: Swarm formation with communication

Ali's thesis involved a swarm of UAVs maintaining a predefined formation. The setup relied on neighbor-to-neighbor communication and was simulated in SITL/ROS. Multi-UAV SITL is significantly harder and demands stronger ROS experience.

  • Neighbor-to-neighbor communication was available
  • Task was closer to true formation maintenance
  • Noted increased complexity with multi-UAV environments

2 Gaps Relative to the Proposal

FYP track: missing elements

  • No treatment of temporary target loss or leader occlusion
  • No explicit relative distance or full pose estimation
  • Task is mainly "keep target in image"
  • No predefined geometric formation
  • No scaling discussion to multiple followers

Thesis track: missing elements

  • Depends on inter-UAV communication
  • Not equivalent to purely visual relative-localization
  • Does not address noisy, sparse, or intermittent visual measurements
I think the starting point should be to combine the single-camera visual tracking idea with explicit relative pose estimation and state filtering, then extend the result to formation control.

3 Refined Questions for Next Meeting

  1. 1
    Is all inter-UAV communication forbidden, or only the communication of absolute pose / GPS-like state?
  2. 2
    Is the follower expected to estimate only bearing and distance, or the full relative pose (6-DOF)?
  3. 3
    What assumptions are acceptable for monocular depth recovery?
    Known size, known 3D model, planar approximation, or learned depth?
  4. 4
    How should scaling to multiple followers be handled?
    Every follower tracking the leader directly, chain topology, leader-plus-neighbor strategy, or another graph structure?
  5. 5
    What is the preferred simulation path?
    Should Gazebo/ROS remain the main environment (matches the proposal), with Simulink used only for control validation?

4 Following Steps

Proposed next steps

  1. Start with one leader and one follower
  2. Use a known leader geometry first (marker or known 3D model)
  3. Estimate relative state from the camera using bearing + depth/pose reconstruction
  4. Add filtering to handle noisy and intermittent measurements
